In my research, I want to help computers understand the meaning of words and utterances, and perform operations that we, humans, perform with language such as logical inference and translation. I want to make them understand that one cannot run and sleep at the same time, that cats are pets but so can be iguanas, that a wonderful view is much better than a good view, and this in different languages.

My main research fields are Computational Linguistics and Natural Language Processing, and I am now growing increasingly interested in cognitive aspects of language learning. My research in natural language semantics addresses both theoretical and application-oriented aspects, ranging from automatically acquired meaning representations to the development of semantic models that can assist NLP applications improve their performance, and automatic evaluation metrics increase their correlation with human quality judgments.

I currently work as Senior Researcher in the Language Technology lab at the University of Helsinki, Department of Digital Humanities, on leave from the French CNRS. I hold an Adjunct appointment in the Computer and Information Science Department at the University of Pennsylvania, Philadelphia (US). I am also an associate researcher in the NLP group of the Athens University of Economics and Business.
